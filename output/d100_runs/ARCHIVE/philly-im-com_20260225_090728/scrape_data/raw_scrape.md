For years, HRT had a reputation for being risky, particularly linked to cancer and cardiovascular events (heart attack, stroke, and blood clots). Many women who might have benefitted from treatment avoided it, while providers were discouraged from prescribing it. Things have changed a lot recently. Expert reviews and new interpretations of long-term data have reversed much of the conventional thinking. Today, HRT is increasingly recognized as a safe and highly beneficial option for many women in perimenopause and menopause. In fact, it not only strengthens bones, it may also reduce cardiovascular risk and improve overall quality of life when prescribed appropriately. At Philadelphia Integrative Medicine, we&#8217;re seeing a sharp rise in questions about HRT, fueled by this growing awareness. Unfortunately, many providers are jumping in without the nuanced knowledge required for proper prescribing, monitoring, and patient support. That&#8217;s where expertise matters.
