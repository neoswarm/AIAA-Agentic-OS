# What's Actually Happening With Your Website

## Bottom Line

Your website has Google's trust but almost no one is clicking through to book appointments. You're educating 350 potential patients every month through search results and AI answers, but Google is keeping them on their platform instead of sending them to you. This is fixable, and you're actually in a better position than most integrative medicine practices.

## What the Data Shows

Your site has an authority score of 10 out of 100. That means Fine credibility with Google, not great but not terrible either. You're visible enough to be trusted, just not dominant.

You attract 350 visitors per month from organic search. That number dropped 19 percent compared to last year. The decline is not about trust. It's about how Google now answers health questions directly without making people click.

You rank for 537 different search terms, and that number grew 12 percent. More keywords means more visibility. The gap is not awareness. The gap is conversion.

You have 174 websites linking to yours. These are votes of confidence in Google's eyes. Enough to stay credible, but not enough to dominate search results in competitive health topics.

Google's AI systems are showing your content in AI Overviews and Gemini answers. That means Google trusts your clinical explanations enough to cite them. But they're citing you without sending patients to you.

## What Changed And Why It Matters

Ten years ago, when someone searched for prediabetes treatment in Plano, Google showed a list of links. The patient clicked your site, read your content, and booked a consultation.

Today, Google reads your content for them. AI Overviews pull the answer directly from your pages and display it at the top of search results. The patient gets educated without ever visiting your site. They learn about root cause medicine, metabolic syndrome, and functional testing from content you created, but Google keeps them in their ecosystem.

This is like writing a detailed patient education handout, giving it to Google, and watching Google hand it out with their name on it while you stand in the background uncredited.

You're not losing trust. You're losing control over the path from education to appointment.

## The Real Problem

You've built a library of helpful clinical content that Google loves. Your explanations of prediabetes reversal, hormone optimization, and integrative approaches are good enough that AI systems reference them.

But here's what's happening. A potential patient searches for chronic fatigue treatment near Plano. Google's AI reads your page, extracts the key points, and serves a perfect summary at the top of the results. The patient reads it, feels informed, and either thinks they can fix it themselves or assumes all functional medicine doctors offer the same thing. They never click through to see your Synergy 4 model, your team-based approach, or your 18-week program.

You're doing the hard work of educating the market while Google captures the relationship. And when that patient is finally ready to book, they search again, see three paid ads from competitors, and click one of those instead.

You're subsidizing patient education for practices that invest in ads instead of content.

## Why This Is Actually an Opportunity

Most integrative medicine and functional medicine practices in the Dallas-Fort Worth area are seeing the same traffic drop you are. Many have worse authority scores and no AI visibility at all. They're invisible in both traditional search and AI answers.

You're different. You have AI trust. Google is already citing your clinical explanations. That's the hardest part to earn, and you've already done it.

The gap is not content quality or trust. The gap is converting that trust into appointments. You need to control the path from AI answer to booked consultation. That means optimizing for the moments when patients are ready to act, not just ready to learn.

You have the visibility and authority to win this. You just need to own the bottom of the funnel where decisions get made.

## If Nothing Changes

If your site stays exactly as it is today, you'll continue educating 350 people per month who never book with you. Over the next year, that's roughly 4,200 potential patients who learn about integrative medicine from your content but convert elsewhere.

If even 2 percent of those people would have been a fit for your Synergy 4 program, you're leaving 84 qualified consultations on the table. At your pricing model, that's somewhere between 840,000 and 2.5 million dollars in uncaptured revenue annually.

Your cost to acquire patients through paid ads will keep rising because you're competing with practices that have worse clinical models but better conversion funnels. You'll pay more per click while your organic traffic continues to decline.

And as more competitors figure out how to convert AI visibility into appointments, you'll lose the positioning advantage you currently have.

## If This Is Handled Correctly

If you optimize for AI-driven patient behavior, you can recapture the demand that's already coming to your content. That means being the practice Google recommends by name when someone asks Gemini for the best integrative medicine doctor in Plano. It means showing up in AI answers with your differentiators clearly stated, not just your clinical explanations.

You can turn your 537 ranking keywords into 1,000-plus by targeting the specific questions patients ask AI systems. Questions like how to reverse prediabetes without medication in Plano or best functional medicine doctor for chronic fatigue near me. These are high-intent searches where patients are ready to book, not just browse.

You can build a conversion path that works even when patients never visit your homepage. That means optimized contact forms on every service page, clear calls to action in content Google is already citing, and a booking funnel that works from mobile AI answer cards.

Realistically, this adds 3 to 5 qualified consultation requests per month from organic search within six months. At your pricing, that's 30,000 to 100,000 dollars in additional annual revenue from traffic you're already attracting but not converting.

And it protects you from the paid ad arms race. When your organic funnel works, you're not dependent on rising ad costs to stay visible.

You already have the trust. You already have the visibility. You just need to own the conversion.
