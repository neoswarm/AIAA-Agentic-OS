ABOUT US - Our Motto

The Integrative Medical Clinic of North Carolina (IMCNC, PLLC) is a leading primary and specialty care multi-disciplinary clinic in North Carolina featuring a host of alternative therapies. The clinic is owned and operated by two highly-trained physicians board-certified in internal medicine and nephrology with specialized training in autoimmune disease and difficult-to-diagnose diseases, in addition to complementary and alternative medicine practices.

The practice embraces alternative, non-pharmaceutical approaches to health, along with the ability to provide pharmaceuticals and proven conventional medicine approaches to support each individual in achieving personalized health.

Contact the clinic directly at (984) 999-0902 to learn more about these services.

At IMC, we aim to H.E.A.L. our community:

H - Holistic: We care for the whole person by taking into account all aspects of health and wellbeing, recognizing each person is on a journey toward living as their best self.

E - Effective: Our delivery of care and approach to healing is rooted in results. We operate in a manner that is effective, efficient, and rooted in empathy.

A - Agents of Change: We empower our patients, our colleagues, and our community to be agents of change by taking personal accountability, being open to innovation, and modeling what is possible.

L - Lifelong Learners: We gain insight from our patients, our colleagues, and our community by using an iterative approach to hone our delivery of care. We learn and continuously improve while educating others along the way.

NEW PATIENTS & BILLING - IMCNC is Accepting New Patients

What is Different About Integrative Medical Care?
Integrative medicine approaches medical concerns with a "whole person" approach. Whole person care is based on the idea that health and disease are connected, and that many factors can affect a person's health including biological makeup, behavior, environment, self-care, lifestyle, and stress management.

What Happens at the First Visit?
At your first visit your Provider will review your answers to the health questions you completed and will ask more questions about the history of the medical issues you are experiencing. Your Provider may offer options for next steps including testing, taking supplements and lifestyle changes.

What Happens at the Second Visit?
Lab review appointment - automatically scheduled. Provider will review all test results and together you and your Provider will determine a treatment plan.

How Do I Become a Patient?
Patients can self-schedule using the "Book An Appointment" button. Shortly after scheduling, you will receive a call from New Patient Coordinator DeMarcus Taylor at (984) 280-3509 or demarcus@imcnorthcarolina.com.

Payment: All patients must keep a valid credit card on file and pay for all appointments in advance.

Insurance: IMCNC does not accept insurance for office visits. Most traditional lab tests, imaging, and prescription medications will be covered by insurance. IMCNC will file out-of-network claims for non-Medicare plans via Reimbursify.

Pricing: New patient visits $400, established patient visits $290 (as of July 1, 2025).

Special: Only Low Dose Allergy (LDA) program in North Carolina, facilitated by Karen Saylor, FNP-BC.
