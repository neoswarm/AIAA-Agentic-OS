Our collaborative team of over 100+ expert doctors, nurse practitioners, and clinicians deliver the highest standard of evidence-based, patient centered care that restores your body’s ability to function, naturally.

Functional Medicine is a shift in how we approach health concerns, illness, and disease. We begin to get to the root cause of your health concerns using science-based medicine to find and treat underlying imbalances. Instead of looking for ways to mask your symptoms, our goal is to restore optimal function throughout your body.

At Aligned Modern Health, our Physical Medicine experts specialize in Chicago’s best non-invasive, non-surgical and drug-free health care.

Chiropractic care is a safe, non-invasive course of treatment that can be used as a first line of defense against pain. Our approach is based on the rehabilitative science of physical medicine. While your initial goal may be to eliminate pain, we go beyond your symptoms to resolve underlying issues and improve your quality of life!

Our team of Chicago acupuncturist blend the best of modern medicine with traditional modalities for evidence-based therapy that treats a wide range of health concerns – including headaches, fertility, stress/anxiety, and gastrointestinal complaints – to name just a few. We approach every patient as an individual and create a customized treatment plan that helps your body heal and balance from within.

We have an amazing team of licensed massage therapists at all of our Chicago locations. Each talented massage therapist in Chicago is ready to assist you in your wellness journey by providing the kind of skilled, rejuvenating therapeutic massage that can reduce stress, ease muscle tension and more.

We build on the time-honored foundation of Traditional Chinese Medicine to power your body’s ability to balance and heal.
Our approach blends technology from modern medicine and applications from the best acupuncturists in the nation.

All of our licensed acupuncturists hold a Master’s degree or Doctorate in Acupuncture and Traditional Chinese Medicine from the top educational institutes in the world. Our Acupuncturists consider all aspects of life, not just physical symptoms, to develop a customized treatment plan, in the context of each patient as a whole person.

Many people ask, how does Acupuncture work? While Acupuncture has been scientifically proven to be effective in treating maladies ranging from pain to seasonal allergies to insomnia, the mechanisms by which it works are still subject to debate and may be described differently by different providers.  The simplest answer, from a modern Western medical perspective, is that tiny Acupuncture needles (less than 1/10th the size of a sewing needle) inserted into the skin promote blood circulation to targeted areas. This in turn activates your body’s immune and nervous systems, reducing inflammation and releasing endorphins to alleviate pain. Acupuncture is rooted in a history that spans more than 2,000 years, and Eastern-based practitioners would probably describe Acupuncture’s effectiveness differently. Acupuncture helps to correct imbalances in the body caused by external factors such as stress, toxins, and injury. Once restored to proper balance, the body can better heal itself.

Small hair-thin needles are inserted into specific points of the body to activate the nervous system and promote blood flow.

Traditional Chinese medicinal herbs are prescribed to support the healing process.

Through a suction technique, glass cups are placed on the body in specific areas to separate the muscles and fascia to promote blood flow and to release toxins in areas of distress.

The Chinese herb Ai Ye (mugwort) is burned over acupuncture points to stimulate their functionality and improve circulation.

A tool is used to scrape and stimulate specific areas of the body to improve circulation and break up scar tissue.

Infrared lamps (heat therapy) are used in conjunction with treatment to increase circulation and support the healing process.

Certain points might receive a small electrical stimulation to help reset your muscles and support neurological conditions.

Benefits are often felt immediately after the first treatment, although the best results are cumulative (as Acupuncture builds on itself as the body returns to a state of health). As treatment progresses, so too does the length of time that its effects last.

Frequency of treatment is dependent on the nature and severity of your condition. Our practitioners are happy to meet with you for a free 15-minute consultation to assess your individual situation and offer a suggested course of treatment.

Acupuncture needles are sterile and as thin as a human hair. They are used only once then disposed of. Most insertions are completely painless, although sometimes patients experience a mild sensation that only lasts a brief moment. Many patients are surprised to find treatments to be very pleasurable, often bringing them into a state of deep relaxation.

Our expert team of doctors, nurse practitioners, clinicians and clinical care coordinators don’t just get you on a path to wellness – they partner with you every step of the way, going beyond a symptom checklist or test result to understand your whole story. You’ll work with a dedicated provider who brings together your symptoms, history, lab results, and goals into one cohesive plan. This level of clinical insight allows us to deliver care that’s not only more personalized, but also more meaningful, more connected, and more likely to result in real change.

At Aligned Modern Health, we believe that great care starts with consistency—and someone who truly knows your story. Our clinical care team includes licensed physicians, nurse practitioners, and coordinators who stay with you, guide your care, and connect the dots over time.

Here’s what that means for you:

1. Time and attention, not rushed care.
Many of our patients come to us after years of not getting real answers, never getting the time or attention they needed to explore the root of their symptoms. We offer deeper evaluations, longer appointments, and care plans that evolve with your life.

2. Expert interpretation, not just information.
You deserve more than data and lab results. Our doctors help you make sense of your labs—translating insights into clear, meaningful action. We lead with education, partnership, and evidence-based guidance.

3. Continuity you can feel.
Health isn’t static—and your care shouldn’t be either. That’s why you’ll work with the same provider across visits. We get to know your baseline, spot patterns, and help you track progress over time. This continuity builds trust, helps us adjust your plan as your needs change, and gives you space to grow.

4. Connected care, not fragmented episodes.
Our clinical team works together across specialties—from Functional Medicine to Hormone Health—to ensure that nothing gets lost in the shuffle. We’re in your details, asking better questions, and creating a path that reflects the full picture of your health.

This is the model behind our virtual care experience. It’s how we help patients feel seen, supported, and truly cared for.

Your care doesn’t stop when the visit ends. Behind the scenes, our doctors Spend time both before and after your visit interpreting your test results and devising your next step so you’re seeing incremental improvements over time.  And your clinical care coordinator is on the front line to help you manage logistics including setting appointments, getting in touch with your doctor and answering all of your questions.
This team-based model helps ensure that every patient receives responsive, high-quality care that moves forward.

Comprehensive testing means uncovering the hidden causes of your symptoms. Our functional medicine doctors look beyond surface-level labs to reveal the full picture of your health

Along with standard blood work, our functional medicine doctors may recommend targeted diagnostic testing, when appropriate, including:

Comprehensive Blood Tests
Assessing key markers of inflammation, metabolic function, cardiovascular risk, and nutrient status.
May include cholesterol and lipid markers, blood sugar and insulin regulation, inflammatory markers, and vitamin levels.

Comprehensive Hormone Analysis
Using blood, saliva, or urine testing to evaluate adrenal, thyroid, and sex hormone balance.
May include cortisol, thyroid hormones, estrogen, progesterone, testosterone, and hormone-binding markers, as well as advanced hormone pattern or metabolism testing when indicated.

Food Sensitivity Testing
Identifying immune reactions to foods that may contribute to inflammation, digestive symptoms, or fatigue.

Comprehensive Stool Analysis
Assessing gut microbiome balance, digestion, inflammation, and potential infections.
May include evaluation of beneficial and pathogenic bacteria, digestive function, and inflammatory markers.

Nutrient Panels
Measuring micronutrient levels essential for energy production, hormone synthesis, immune health, and recovery.

Environmental Exposure Testing
Detecting environmental stressors that may interfere with metabolic or hormonal health.
May include assessment for heavy metals, mold-related toxins, or other exposures when clinically appropriate.

Each of these tools gives a unique window into how your body is working. Taken together, they provide a whole-body view that helps your provider identify root causes and create a precise treatment plan.

Many health conditions share overlapping symptoms—fatigue, brain fog, digestive distress, weight changes, mood swings. Without thorough testing, these signals can be misinterpreted or treated only at the surface level, leaving the true cause unaddressed.

Comprehensive testing provides clarity:

Negative or “normal” results are just as valuable as positive ones—they narrow the focus and prevent wasted effort on the wrong path.

Comprehensive testing does not mean ordering every test available or running broad panels without clear purpose. More testing does not automatically lead to better answers. Instead, comprehensive testing is guided by clinical reasoning. Tests are selected based on your symptoms, health history, and goals, and interpreted in context—so results lead to clarity, not confusion. Just as important as knowing what to test is knowing what not to test. This approach helps avoid unnecessary expense, reduces conflicting or misleading results, and keeps care focused on the insights that actually move your health forward.

Think of functional medicine testing like detective work. Each test result is a clue. The more clues your provider has, the closer they can get to identifying the real culprit behind your symptoms. Sometimes answers come quickly. Other times, it’s a process of testing, treatment, and re-evaluation. While that may sound tedious, the true frustration is going months or years without clarity. Comprehensive testing keeps you moving toward answers.

Lab results don’t speak for themselves. A patient Googling their test results can easily misinterpret what’s happening. Functional medicine doctors are trained to interpret results through a different lens—functional ranges that identify imbalances long before disease is formally diagnosed.

Can I do comprehensive testing as a telehealth patient?

Yes. Through our partnerships with national laboratories and the use of mail-in diagnostic kits, functional medicine testing can be completed from anywhere. This makes comprehensive testing fully accessible for our virtual care patients, with results seamlessly shared and interpreted by your provider.

Does comprehensive testing take more time?
Some specialized tests require outside labs and can take longer to process than conventional blood work. But “comprehensive” doesn’t mean more samples or more visits—it means broader insight from the same or similar testing processes. The extra time is in the analysis, not the burden on you.

Is functional medicine testing more expensive than conventional testing?
Some tests are not covered by insurance and carry higher upfront costs. However, many patients find the long-term investment worthwhile. By identifying and addressing root causes early, comprehensive testing can help reduce reliance on ongoing medications, procedures, and hospital visits. The goal is not short-term symptom management, but long-term health outcomes.

Will my insurance cover comprehensive testing?
Coverage varies by plan and by test. Some blood work may be billed through insurance, while more specialized tests may not be. Our care team reviews costs upfront so you can make informed decisions without surprises.

How do I know which tests I actually need?
Not every patient needs every test. Your provider recommends testing based on your symptoms, history, and goals. The goal is precision—not overload—so you receive the most relevant insights without unnecessary testing.

Comprehensive testing is where clarity begins. It allows us to see the full picture of your health, rule out guesswork, and build a plan tailored to your needs. Whether your journey is straightforward or requires multiple steps, our functional medicine providers are committed to walking it with you—using testing as the foundation for answers, insight, and lasting health.
